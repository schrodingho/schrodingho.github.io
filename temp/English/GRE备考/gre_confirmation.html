IDENTIFICATION POLICY

Identification requirements are strictly enforced. If you arrive at the test center without the required ID, you will not be admitted and your test fee will be forfeited. It is your responsibility to read and understand the ID requirements in the GRE Bulletin or on the GRE website at https://www.ets.org/gre/revised_general/test_day/id.

Residents of Mainland China must present a valid Second Generation National Resident ID (effective January 1, 2013). Citizens of Taiwan must present their Travel Permit to Mainland China. Citizens of Hong Kong and Macau must present their Resident Identity Card. Citizens from all other countries and locations must present a valid passport.

ON TEST DAY

Report to the test center at least 30 minutes before your scheduled appointment for check-in procedures. If you arrive late, you may not be admitted, and your test fee will be forfeited. Plan to be at the test center approximately 5 hours.

You must bring a valid photo identification with signature (see Identification Policy above).

Cell phones, smartphones (e.g. Blackberry(R) or iPhone(R) devices), PDAs, and other electronic recording, listening or photographic devices are not permitted in the testing center. For further information see the GRE Information and Registration Bulletin.

RESCHEDULE / CANCEL POLICY

You must change or cancel your test online at https://gre.neea.edu.cn or https://gre.neea.cn no later than ten full days prior to your appointment (not including the day of the test or the day of your request), or your test fee will be forfeited.

If you change your test date, you will be charged a rescheduling fee of RMB¥ 388.00 . If you cancel your test no later than ten full days prior to your test date, you will receive a refund equivalent to half of your original test fee.

Review all reschedule and cancellation policies at https://www.ets.org/gre/revised_general/register/change.

You can take the computer-delivered GRE revised General Test once every 21- days, up to five times within any continuous rolling 12-month period (365 days). This applies even if you canceled your scores on a test taken previously.

TEST PREPARATION MATERIALS

Registrants in the U.S., Puerto Rico and Canada are advised to download the free POWERPREP® II Version 1.0 software available at https://www.ets.org/gre/revised_general/prepare to prepare for the computer-based GRE revised General Test.

The free POWERPREP® II software can be downloaded at anytime from the GRE website at https://www.ets.org/gre/powerprep2 to help you prepare for the computer-based GRE revised General Test. Additional test preparation information is available at https://www.ets.org/gre/prepare.

ETS® PERSONAL POTENTIAL INDEX

The ETS Personal Potential Index (ETS® PPI) helps you show graduate and business school programs a more complete picture of your potential for success. It provides schools with more evidence of a broader range of your capabilities - including knowledge and creativity, communication skills and teamwork - than is captured in grades, test scores and recommendation letters. As a GRE revised General Test registrant, you can send four FREE ETS PPI Evaluation Reports. Visit www.ets.org/ppi and get started today.
